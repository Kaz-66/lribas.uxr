PlunetBoard - A New Dashboard for the Service

The Plunet Product team decided to create a new dashboard (named “PlunetBoard”), taking advantage of modern technologies like REST API and Angular Material, and updating the old design. The project lasted several months and involved close teamwork between the design team, product owners, and both front-end and back-end developers.

I participated at different points in the project, my most significant contributions being research using focus group to define use cases, early usability testing and validation, and designing effective time filters.

Using Focus Group to Define Use Cases

Context

REST API technology allowed us to connect data space that was previously out of reach. To propose a new dashboard that meets users' needs, it was essential to determine what data they wanted to access and how they wanted it to be displayed.

Process

During the 2022 Plunet Conference, we organized a focus group with 22 participants. Alongside the design team, we hosted a workshop where participants discussed their ideas, suggested widgets they'd like to see, and indicated the ones they found most relevant. My main role was analyzing the data from this workshop.

Results

The results highlighted the main themes for our users, such as Project Manager (PM), Management, and Finance. Frequency of appearance analysis supported us to prioritize widget development.

Early Usability Testing and Validation

Context

We created an initial Minimum Viable Product to test the technology and basic user flows. I performed the first round of usability tests to spot any early issues and confirm that basic user flows—like adding, removing, and organizing widgets—were obvious for the users.

Process

I ran a series of moderated usability tests with five participants to identify usability issues. After each test, participants completed a standard System Usability Scale (SUS) questionnaire, giving us a validation score and a way to track usability improvements over time.

Results

The results showed the interface is intuitive, with an A+ SUS score, and helped identify some early usability issues.

Designing Effective Time Filters

Context

A key feature of the new dashboard is the ability to filter data displayed based on time (by creation date, due date, etc). Designing time filter involved two challenges: defining the relevant filters and how to display them intuitively.

Process

We worked in two stages with the design team. First, we sent out asurvey to determine the important milestones for time filters. Then, we held a design workshop to explore various design possibilities.

Results

The survey revealed that users are most interested in short-term milestones, and that hour-based filtering is crucial for a significant part of them.

We opted for a compact dropdown design with limited advanced customization to keep the screen uncluttered, given the filter system’s existing complexity.
