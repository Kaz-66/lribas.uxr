Time to Think - Take back control of your screen time

This project was undertaken as part of a bootcamp, where I was tasked with designing an application to address a need I identified in my surroundings.

The fictional company, National Wellness Institute, requested that I design a digital tool to enhance the health and wellness of phone users.

I chose to create a screen time management application, as excessive phone usage is a common issue I frequently observe. I strongly believe this problem is, and will continue to be, significant in our modern societies.

The goal of this project is to propose a solution to the question: How can we help people manage their smartphone usage?

Exploratory Research

The goals of the research was to:

  • Confirm the relevance of the project
  • Determine who the target users are
  • Define key user pain points

The research process included:

  • Semi-structured user interview (5 participants)
  • Competitive analysis
  • Define key user pain points

Relevance of the Project

There is a public health problem with
the excessive phone use

  • Data show that excessive phone use can lead to headaches, decreased attention, shortness of temper, sleep disorders and depression.
  • 66% of the population shows signs of nomophobia (phone addiction).

There is a desire among many people to reduce
the time spent on their phone

  • 5 out of 5 people interviewed reported they felt that their phone use was sometimes excessive, and they would like to reduce it.
  • There are over 1,000,000 downloads for the most popular phone screen time management apps on Google Play.

The market misses innovative and effective solutions

  • The apps on Google Play offered little new functionalities compared to the screen time management options in Android system.

User Profiles

My project focuses on populations that are more affected by phone use. These are mainly active workers (between 18 and 45 years old) and urban populations, having daily contact with digital and social networks.

User Pain Points

Long periods of use and compulsive application access
are the most problematic usages

  • All participants reported two main issues: prolonged phone use, which causes negative feelings like time-wasting, and compulsive app access, leading to loss of concentration and frustration.

Gaming, social networks, messengers and dating apps
are the most likely to lead to problematic behaviours

  • All participants report that problematic uses are primarily gaming, dating, social networks and messengers apps.
  • These results are correlated with internet information (39% of phone use are done on Social media, 10% gaming and 10% messaging application).

Time limit and usage notifications
are not very effective features

  • Participants report that they do not want to add a time limit on use because it gives a feeling of loss of control over their phone, which makes this measure too coercive.
  • On the other hand, participants report notifications do not bother them enough. They turn them off by reflex, which makes them totally ineffective.

Define

Persona

The Persona is Bryan Egan, a person who has problems with his use of gaming and dating apps.

Problem Statements

Based on my research data and my own hypotheses, I have established these two problem statements:

Phone users need an innovative tool to better manage their phone usage,
as they feel it's causing them to lose time and focus.

The innovative tool should offer features that are more limiting than notifications,
yet less stringent than a strict time limit

Ideation

Behavioral Model

To guide my ideation session, I created a simple behavioral model in graph form. This model outlines the user's emotions and actions while using an application, helping to pinpoint where I can influence behavior.

Selected features

I decided to include the basic functions already present in other applications:

  • Customizable dashboard - The application will offer a customizable visualization of application usage
  • Set up a time frame - The user will be able to choose when the restrictions will apply.
  • Set up your own objective - The user will be enable to set his own reduction target
  • Restriction per app - The restrictions can be apply only on a specific application (or on an application group).

Additionaly, the value proposition of the Time to Think application will be based on these unique features:

  • Ask confirmation to open application - When opening the application targeted by this restriction, the user will have to go through an additional step he will have to confirm that he wants to open the application.
  • Request a boring task to unlock or continue to use the application - This is the core functionality of the application. When opening an application targeted by a restriction, the user will have to perform a boring but simple task. The duration of the task can be variable.
  • Colors indication - With color indicators, the closer they get to their time limit, the more the screen's colors will fade, eventually turning black and white.

Visual Design

The visual design is centered around the two key benefits the application offers: time control and the serenity it provides.
