WeResearch - Linking Research and Start-ups

This project, completed during a bootcamp, is based on a market need identified by a stakeholder.

WeResearch is a future website designed to connect researchers with startup founders and executives for collaboration.

I started this project when WeResearch was funded, with the stakeholder's goal of attracting more investors. My focus was on designing the main pages of the website to make the concept more tangible for potential investors.

In this project, I propose a solution to the challenge of fostering partnerships between startup owners and researchers.

Project Scope

Status of the Project WeResearch

At the start of the project, when WeResearch was funded, the core features—profile creation, search tool, matching algorithm, and coaching system—were already outlined, along with a growth roadmap.
The stakeholder's goal was to attract more investors, so my objective was to design the main pages of the site to present to them and lay the groundwork for the first Minimum Viable Product (MVP).
Initially, I had access to the project's presentation documents. Only a site mock-up existed, with design and content yet to be defined.

Prototype Content

The prototype content should closely resemble the MVP of the WeResearch site.

To be functional, the site must allow users to create accounts, have profiles for both researchers and startups, and enable them to connect. Additionally, a landing page is needed to introduce the site to both users and investors.

Based on these requirements, I chose to design the following pages:

  • The landing page
  • The registration process page
  • The profile page for researchers
  • The profile page for startup companies
  • The search tool

Explorative Research

The research process included:

  • A survey conducted by the stakeholder before the project (n=13; researchers only)
  • A survey conducted by me (n=22; 12 researchers and 10 startup employees)
  • Two interviews with the stakeholder
  • Semi-structured interviews (n=5; 4 researchers and 1 former startup founder)

Researcher - Goals & pain points

Researchers are looking to collaborate with start-up owners because:

  • They want to work on impactful project
  • They are interested in gaining work experience outside academic area

But...

  • It is difficult to have a successful communication with the start-up's management
  • They don’t know how they can contribute to a start-up project
  • They don't know where to look for projects and structure to collaborate with.

Startup Owners - Goals & pain points

Start-up owners are looking to collaborate with researchers because:

  • The researcher expertise is beneficial for product the development
  • This increases the likelihood to get an innovative product

But...

  • Find researchers is time consuming and require good expertise
  • Websites to recruit academic researchers are not adapted to companies’ need
  • The working methods of researchers differ from those of start-ups and make collaboration challenging

Define

Problem Statement

I based my work on two problem statements, one for each user type.

"Startup owners need a platform to efficiently find researchers who meet their specific needs,
as recruiting the right talent is often time-consuming and unproductive."

"Researchers need a platform to connect with startup owners,
as their limited industry knowledge can hinder successful research and collaboration."

Visual Design

Moodboard

Creating a brand identity for WeResearch is a crucial aspect of my project, as it helps users easily recognize and understand the website.

I based my moodboard on three key attributes agreed upon with the stakeholder: Transdisciplinarity, Innovation, and Professionalism. These concepts will shape the brand's identity.

Style tile

I chose three reference colors for the website: blue for research, light blue-green for startups, and a contrasting pale orange to highlight key actions.

For typography, I selected the clean and modern "Work Sans" for titles, paired with "Helvetica" to ensure good readability of the text.

Prototype

Design Process

I followed a classic design process. For each page, I brainstormed multiple design possibilities on paper, selected the most suitable one, and then refined it to a high-fidelity level.

User Testing

Since the functionalities in this prototype are common (user profiles, registration flow, search tool), I decided to test the prototype at the high-fidelity level.

Through user testing, I was able to enhance the textual content, improve the user interface, and refine the brand image.

Next Steps and Limits

My work represents the initial stage of an iterative process, and there are several important next steps to consider. It would be advantageous for me to work on the different functions and pages of the MVP independently and later integrate them into a cohesive whole.

User Data Limitations

In constructing personas and defining user problems, my research was limited by time and the scope of my contacts. The data is skewed, with researchers making up about 70% of my sample, which introduces a bias, especially since my stakeholder is also a researcher.

To refine my understanding, it would be valuable to conduct on-site interviews at startups collaborating with researchers. In-depth case studies, balanced with broader surveys, would help establish a more solid knowledge base.

Explore Engine

The current version of the explore engine is simple but sufficient for the MVP. The search system is straightforward, and no usability issues were identified during testing. However, it is crucial to verify whether the filters align with users' mental models and are effective in practice. These filters should ideally be refined based on user data from a Beta or Alpha release to better understand user behavior.

An interesting development idea came from a user testing participant, suggesting that users should be able to explore projects, not just profiles. Projects could be proposed by companies or researchers, which would add value and align with user needs, particularly for researchers.
